Abstract

Perceptions of others are formed partly through individual nonverbal cues like facial expressions or body language. These nonverbal cues help form more accurate perceptions of others, which are fundamental in situations where information is lacking. We exploit a startup financing context, whereby equity investors assess entrepreneurial projects they know little about. We propose a deep learning method that is able to extract (investor) perceptions of personal characteristics of an entrepreneur from a video of his/her startup pitch. Our model consists of a Spatio-temporal Graph Convolutional Network that is trained on a set of 218 monocular videos of startup pitches. We use a human pose estimator to obtain speaker's 3D skeletal information and then construct spatio-temporal graphs to use as input for our network. We survey 1,772 independent raters to obtain perceptions of entrepreneurs, which we use as ground truth. Results outperform existing learning techniques using hand-crafted features. We finally explore real effects and show that the output generated by our model significantly explains actual investment decisions. The code, the trained model, and the dataset are publicly available at https://ipcv.github.io/stgcnNonverbal
